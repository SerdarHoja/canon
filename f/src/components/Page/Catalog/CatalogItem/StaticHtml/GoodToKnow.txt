                    <h1>
                        <a name="RDCDWI" id="RDCDWI">⁠</a>Reverse Encoding Distortion
                        <br/>
                        Correction (RDC DWI)
                    </h1>
                    <div>
                        Angela Marin
                    </div>
                </div>

                <div class="col-6 col-sm-2">
                            <span>
                                    <img
                                            src="https://canonmedical.widen.net/content/r7e6rt2mto/original/G2K_RDCDWI_image01.png?u=cglmil&amp;"

    <div class="section-20">
        <div class="ProductDetailContent ContentArea">

                </div>
                <div class="col-sm-12">
                    <div>
                        <span style="font-size: 16pt;">WHAT?</span>
                        <br/>
                        Reverse Encoding Distortion Correction (RDC) is a technique to reduce geometric distortion
                        in Diffusion Weighted Imaging (DWI).
                        <br/>
                        <br/>
                        <span style="font-size: 16pt;">WHY?</span>
                        <br/>
                        The Echo Planar Imaging (EPI) technique, often used in DWI, is susceptible to
                        distortions, especially in the phase encoding direction.
                        <br/>
                        <br/>
                        <span style="font-size: 16pt;">WHEN?</span>
                        <br/>
                        To reduce distortions in DWI while maintaining a short scan time.

                    </div>
                </div>
                <div class="col-sm-12">

                    <h1>
                        <a name="IMC" id="IMC">⁠</a>Iterative Motion Correction (IMC)
                    </h1>
                    <div>
                        Wissam AlGhuraibawi, PhD
                    </div>


                </div>

                <div class="col-6 col-sm-2">

                            <span>
                                    <img src="https://canonmedical.widen.net/content/ed2ltnpusx/original/G2K_IMC_image01.png?u=cglmil&amp;"
                                         class="img-fluid">

    <div class="section-20">
        <div class="ProductDetailContent ContentArea">
            <div class="row">
                <div class="col-sm-12">
                </div>
                <div class="col-sm-12">
                    <div>
                        <span style="font-size: 16pt;">WHAT?</span>
                        <br>
                        IMC is a Deep Learning-based motion correction method to improve the quality of images affected
                        by patient motion.
                        <br>
                        <br>
                        <span style="font-size: 16pt;">WHY?</span>
                        <br>
                        Patient motion creates artifacts that can cause images to lose their diagnostic value and may
                        necessitate a repeated scan.
                        <br>
                        <br>
                        <span style="font-size: 16pt;">WHEN?</span>
                        <br>
                        IMC can reduce imaging artifacts from sporadic, rigid, and nonrigid patient motion in fast
                        spin-echo (FSE) sequences. IMC supports multiple imaging weightings such as T2, T1 IR, STIR
                        &amp; FLAIR, and in any plane for brain and cervical spine examinations.

                    </div>

                    <h1>
                        <a name="MetalArtifactReduction" id="MetalArtifactReduction">⁠</a>Metal Artifact Reduction
                    </h1>
                    <div>
                        Erin J. Kelly, PhD
                    </div>


                </div>

                <div class="col-6 col-sm-2">

                            <span>
                                    <img src="https://canonmedical.widen.net/content/zremqy3enh/original/G2K_Erin-Kelly_image.png?u=cglmil&amp;"

                <div class="col-sm-12">
                    <div>
                        <span style="font-size: 16pt;">WHAT?</span>
                        <br>
                        Metal artifact reduction strategies and sequences are designed to reduce susceptibility
                        artifacts caused by the presence of metal in an MR imaging volume.
                        <br>
                        <br>
                        <span style="font-size: 16pt;">WHY?</span>
                        <br>
                        In MRI, most metallic implants cause artifacts which can interfere with the anatomy of interest
                        in the image by causing distortion or signal loss, due primarily to strong magnetic
                        susceptibility.
                        <br>
                        <br>
                        <span style="font-size: 16pt;">WHEN?</span>
                        <br>
                        In patients with metallic implants, artifact reduction techniques are especially useful for
                        reducing the interference of related artifacts thus facilitating a more confident diagnosis.

                    </div>


                </div>

                <div class="col-6 col-sm-2">

                            <span>

                <div class="col-sm-12">
                    <div>
                        <span style="font-size: 16pt;">WHAT?</span>
                        <br>
                        A family of MR angiography techniques that utilize fresh spins to depict the contrast in blood
                        vessels as an alternative to an injection of an exogenous contrast agent. In particular, Fresh
                        Blood Imaging (FBI), Flow Spoiled FBI (FS-FBI) and Time-Spatial Labeling Inversion Pulse
                        (Time-SLIP), are discussed.
                        <br>
                        <br>
                        <span style="font-size: 16pt;">WHY?</span>
                        <br>
                        Non-contrast angiography (NC-MRA) provides the safest option for MR Angiography. It can
                        exquisitely depict arteries and veins throughout the body in 3 dimensions, while eliminating the
                        need for gadolinium-based contrast agents.
                        <br>
                        <br>
                        <span style="font-size: 16pt;">WHEN?</span>
                        <br>
                        Any time visualization of the arteries or veins is needed for clinical diagnosis and follow-up
                        related to narrowing or blockage of blood vessels, NC-MRA can be used for evaluation. NC-MRA is
                        especially important for those patients who have a contraindication to exogenous contrast
                        agents.
